**File:** `India_CPI_Case_Study.xlsx`

### üìã Project Overview

* **Data Cleaning:** Cleaned raw government datasets, handled null values, and standardized date formats.

## 2. US Healthcare Case Study
**File:** `US_Healthcare_Case_Study.xlsx`

### üìã Project Overview
A deep dive into US Healthcare data focusing on patient demographics, treatment costs, and hospital efficiency. The goal was to identify cost-saving opportunities and patterns in patient care.

### üõ†Ô∏è Key Features
* **Dynamic Dashboard:** Built an interactive dashboard using **Slicers** and **Pivot Charts** for real-time filtering.
* **Cost Modeling:** Used **Advanced Formulas** (SUMIFS, XLOOKUP) to calculate average costs per treatment type.
* **Data Validation:** Implemented data validation rules to ensure data integrity during entry.

### üìä Key Insights
* Discovered that Emergengy admission type is more costly than the other admission types.						
* The Summer  season records the heighest overall patient volume with 3366 total admissions.		
* Winter shows the lowest patient inflow, with only 1615 total admissions,which is less than half of the Summer peak.		
*Findings : Hypertension is the most common medical condition, accounting for 2155 total admissions.		
*Cancer shows a balanced but high demand across all types, with 1647 total patient needing a mix of elective, emergency, and urgent care.
*Findings : Hypertension is the most common medical condition, accounting for 2155 total admissions.				
*Hypertension also records the heighest number of Emergency caes (993) and Urgent (905).				
*Obesity (925) and  Arithritis (867)  are the primary derivers for Elective  admissions, suggesting these are largely planned procedures or consultations.				
*Asthma shows a high reliance on Urgent care, with 820 admissions in the category compared to only 213  elective caese.				
*Cancer shows a balanced but high demand across all types, with 1647 total patient needing a mix of elective, emergency, and urgent care.
